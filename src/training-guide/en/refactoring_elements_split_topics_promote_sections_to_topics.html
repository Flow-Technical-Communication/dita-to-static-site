Refactoring elements > split topics (promote sections to topics)

You may have written a topic which consists of multiple sections, but for some reason, you want to split that topic up into multiple child topics and thus "promote" each section title to a topic title. You may want to do this, for example:

  • Because the topic is too long and you just remembered the chunking principle of structured authoring
  • Because you want to reuse the content of one of more sections in another DITA map
  1. In your DITA map, right-click the topic (topicref) which you want to split.
  2. Choose Refactoring > Convert sections to new topics.
  3. In the XML Refactoring dialog, select Selected references.
  4. Click Finish.
Your selected topic will be split in 3 or more files:
  • A "parent" topic a, which only consists of the title and an empty <*body> element
  • Two or more "child" topics b (depending on the number of sections), in which your previous section titles have now been promoted to topic titles.
Remove the empty <*body> in the parent topic to avoid a blank space below the title in your PDF file.
