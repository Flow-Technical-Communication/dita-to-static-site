Prolog information

The <prolog> element contains information about the topic as a whole:

  • Author: <author>
  • Audience: <audience>
  • Publisher: <publisher>
  • Source: <source>
  • Product information: <brand>, <prodinfo>, <prodname>, <prognum>, <series>, <vrm>, <vrmlist> and <featnum>
  • Classification: <category> and <component>
  • Copyright information: <copyright>, <copyrholder> and <copyryear>
  • Critical tracking dates: <created>, <revised> and <critdates>
  • Permissions for use/management of the content: <permissions>
  • Key words and index terms related to the topic: <keywords>
  • Extensive metadata about the content of the document: <metadata> and <othermeta>
  • A resource-id that allows a topic to be associated with external resources such as linking to programming components as contextual help: <resourceid>

Much of these elements will not be displayed with the topic in the output, but separate, for example in PDF introduction pages or page information.

Introductory topic information

Abstracts

DITA provides an element to include abstracts. The <abstract> element contains the initial content of a topic:
  • Paragraphs providing a summary of the contents of the topic
  • One or more <shortdesc> elements providing link previews or summaries

Short descriptions

The <shortdesc> is placed between the topic title and the topic body. It is used for:
  • Representation of the purpose or theme of the topic
  • Link preview
  • Searching
The element is optional, but useful and thus recommended.
